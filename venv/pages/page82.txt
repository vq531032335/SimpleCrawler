<td width="70%" style="padding:1em 1em 1em 1em; border:1px solid #107020; background-color:#F0FFEA" valign="top">
<p><span style="font-size:16pt">Good articles in Wikipedia</span><br />
</p>
<div class="floatleft"><a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_support_vote.svg" class="image"><img alt="Symbol support vote.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/94/Symbol_support_vote.svg/64px-Symbol_support_vote.svg.png" width="64" height="66" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/94/Symbol_support_vote.svg/96px-Symbol_support_vote.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/94/Symbol_support_vote.svg/128px-Symbol_support_vote.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185" /></a></div> 
<p>A <b>good article</b> (GA) is an article that meets a core set of editorial standards but is not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FA">featured article</a> quality. Good articles meet the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_article_criteria" title="Wikipedia:Good article criteria">good article criteria</a>, passing through the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_article_nominations" title="Wikipedia:Good article nominations">good article nomination process</a> successfully. They are well written, contain factually accurate and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:V" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:V">verifiable</a> information, are broad in coverage, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPOV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPOV">neutral in point of view</a>, stable, and illustrated, where possible, by relevant <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Images" title="Wikipedia:Images">images</a> with suitable <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Image_copyright_tags" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Image copyright tags">copyright licenses</a>. Good articles do not have to be as comprehensive as featured articles, but they should not omit any major facets of the topic: a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Compare_criteria_Good_v._Featured_article" title="Wikipedia:Compare criteria Good v. Featured article">comparison</a> of the criteria for good and featured articles describes further differences.
</p><p>Currently, out of the <b>5,782,875</b> articles on Wikipedia, <b>29,060</b> are categorized as good articles (about 1 in 199), most of which are listed below. An additional <b>5,434</b> are listed as featured articles (about 1 in 1,070) and <b>3,425</b> as featured lists (about 1 in 1,690). Because articles are only included on one list, a good article that has been promoted to featured status is removed from the good articles list. Adding good and featured articles and lists together gives a total of <b>37,919</b> articles (about 1 in 153). A small plus sign inside a circle (<a href="/wiki/File:Symbol_support_vote.svg" class="image" title="This symbol designates good articles on Wikipedia."><img alt="This symbol designates good articles on Wikipedia." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/94/Symbol_support_vote.svg/15px-Symbol_support_vote.svg.png" width="15" height="15" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/94/Symbol_support_vote.svg/23px-Symbol_support_vote.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/94/Symbol_support_vote.svg/30px-Symbol_support_vote.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="180" data-file-height="185" /></a>) in the top-right corner of an article's page indicates that the article is good.
</p><p>The process for designating an article as a good article is intentionally straightforward. Any editor, preferably one who creates or contributes to an article, who believes that the article meets the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_article_criteria" title="Wikipedia:Good article criteria">good article criteria</a>, may <b>nominate</b> the article for an impartial reviewer to assess. Another editor may <b>review</b> the article after selecting it from a queue of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_article_nominations" title="Wikipedia:Good article nominations">good article nominations</a> then evaluating it against the good article criteria. If it is accepted by the reviewer, the nominated article is added to the list of good articles below. Anyone may nominate or review an article by following the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_article_nominations/Instructions" title="Wikipedia:Good article nominations/Instructions">instructions</a>. Similarly, anyone who believes that an article no longer meets the good article criteria may propose to <b>delist</b> it by following the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_article_reassessment" title="Wikipedia:Good article reassessment">instructions for reassessment</a>.
</p><p>More good content is available at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Good_topics" title="Wikipedia:Good topics">Wikipedia:Good topics</a>.
</p>
