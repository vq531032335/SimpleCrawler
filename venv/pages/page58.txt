						<h3>General</h3>
						<ul>
							<li><i>Q: What is Steam Translation?</i><br>The Steam Translation Server (STS) is a project in which Steam and selected games can be translated by volunteers.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: What can be translated on Steam Translation?</i><br>Currently all localization files of Steam, Valve games, certain third party games and mods can be translated. Other third party games, as for example GMOD, are not available at the time being.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Who can take part in the translations?</i><br>Anyone with a non-limited account who maintains a good VAC status. It is required to create a Steam Community profile to participate.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: How do I stop the little red light under the messages tab from blinking?</i><br>Please click on the messages tab and try to find an 'OK' button next to one of the announcements on that page. Please click that button to stop the blinking. If it does not stop or you have no 'OK' button, then please let us know: <a href="mailto:translationserver@valvesoftware.com">translationserver@valvesoftware.com</a><br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Does Steam Translation have any confidentiality rules?</i><br>While there are no confidentiality rules or Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDA) for Steam Translation, please be considerate in not spoiling or leaking potentially critical information or strings.<br>If you feel the nagging urge to leak and spoil, we are here to help and can always mediate your nag: <a href="mailto:translationserver@valvesoftware.com">CanILeakThis?@valvesoftware.com</a>.<br><br>

							<li><i>Q: Do I have to apply/register to be able to suggest translations?</i><br>Yes, before you can suggest translations you will have to undergo a two-step application procedure after you log on above.<br>Please note: small corrections can also be <a href="mailto:translationserver@valvesoftware.com">mailed</a> or posted on the <a href="http://steamcommunity.com/groups/STSLounge/discussions" target="_blank">STS Lounge</a>.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Can I apply for moderator status?</i><br>No, you cannot directly apply for moderator status. However, in certain third party realms on STS translators of your group can vote for you. If you get enough votes, you will be considered for moderator permissions.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Can I translate for multiple languages?</i><br>You should focus your efforts on one language.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Will you be adding any additional languages to STS?</i><br>We do appreciate your interest in the Steam Translation Server and improving Steam for users in your region. However, we currently aren't looking into adding any further languages to STS.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Can we translate images (e.g. for the Steam community)?</i><br>Not at the moment.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: So I joined Steam Translation, but sometimes I don't have the time, or a temporary lack of motivation to participate. Will I be removed, if I don't do anything?</i><br>Once your initial application is accepted, you will always be welcome back to contribute, without the need to apply again. 
						</ul>
						<h3>Translating</h3>
						<ul>
							<li><i><a name="more_suggestions">Q: Why do I only have 25 (125, 250, 500...) suggestions?</a> I'm out of suggestions?</i><br>Please be patient: Once 25 of your translations are accepted you will get bumped up to 125 suggestions, once you gather 125 you will receive 250 suggestions (and so on 500, 1000...). In the beginning this might be an obstacle, but as you have to get used to Steam Translation, we have to get used to any new applicants, too. In a few days or weeks you surely won't encounter any suggestion limit anymore.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: How do I know my suggestion has been approved/declined?</i><br>You can check your own user_activity page (under the Reports tab) and click on your name to retrieve a list in the 'translate' section sorted by the status of your own suggestions.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Is there a penalty if my suggestions get declined?</i><br>No, but if your suggestions consistently are under par your participation on Steam Translation might come to an end.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: How can I chat with other translators?</i><br>You can join the Steam Community group for your language and you will find a status of active translators in your group's chat room in the 'translate' section.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: I think a suggestion needs to be improved, can I edit it?</i><br>You cannot edit anyone's suggestion, but you can hit the 'EDIT' button and the original suggestion loads into the text field. Once you are done please hit 'SUBMIT' and your fixes will be added as a separate, new suggestion.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: How long does it take to get my translation reviewed?</i><br>There is no set schedule and it depends on how active your moderators are.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: How do I remove a suggestion?</i><br>You can only remove your own suggestion (use the button offered under your suggestion). Moderators cannot remove a user's suggestion. Admins can, but mostly will only remove a suggestion that has been declined by a moderator.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: What to do when I find a text that is not translated and cannot be found on STS?</i><br>Please email us: <a href="mailto:translationserver@valvesoftware.com">translationserver@valvesoftware.com</a> including a screenshot and an exact description of the issue you encountered.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: When will my translation be visible online? If I approve a translation, will it be immediately visible?</i><br>There is no set schedule, but usually we try to push updated translations live with any Steam and game update. Once a moderator approves a suggestion an admin still has to apply and export new suggestions before they eventually can be part of an update and go live.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: When will you update translation files? Why is this file missing/incomplete?</i><br>Some files are still missing, but eventually will be added to STS. You can be notified when new strings are added by opting-in to email notifications (in your User Activity page) or following @STSUpdate on Twitter.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Who is applying my translations?</i><br>Steam Translation Administrators working at Valve.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Can you send me a translation file for local testing?</i><br>You can export 5 files per day using the "Export Files" section on the "Reports" tab - it includes some options to export only updated/untranslated strings or to export suggestions not yet applied as well.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: I saw a suggestion I liked but I'm not a moderator, so I can't approve it directly. What can I do?</i><br>You can click on the +1 vote button to vote for that suggestion. Then a +1 will be shown in the suggestion. You can also click the +X number to see who else voted for that suggestion.
							<li><i>Q: I'm exporting localization files and am testing them locally, but Steam always overwrites my files. Please help?</i><br>Try launching Steam from the commandline with option -noverifyfiles passed to it or add this parameter to your shortcut for Steam. This should prevent these files to be overwritten.
						</ul>
						<a id="Core_Teams"><h3>Core Teams</h3></a>
						<ul>
							<li><i>Q: What are Core Teams?</i><br>Core Teams represent the most active translators per month and language and should give you an overview of your overall performance and your progression within your language team.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: How are the monthly numbers for the team effort per words and per suggestions calculated?</i><br>These monthly numbers are the sum of past month's top 5 translators per language divided by a divisor per language.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: What happens if I meet the monthly team effort numbers?</i><br>If you are in the top 5 of the past month you level up, and you also level up every time you achieve these monthly numbers. If more than 10 translators chime in on this effort, the ranking is ordered by word count.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: What about someone else editing my suggestion. Do I lose the word count for my translation?</i><br>Generally the notion of translation ownership is a misconception while contributing on Steam Translation. Translations evolve and change. There is no judgment attached to someone else altering your suggestion.<br>Specifically for the Core Team feature, the word count of !untranslated! English texts is counted and split between two or more translators, if the string is longer than 10 words and the edited suggestion(s) are more than 75% similar to the original suggestion.<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Can I achieve a Translator's Badge by leveling up on STS?</i><br>Yes. Translators reaching level 12 on Steam Translation will be granted the Translator's Badge (e.g. staying in your languages top 5 for 6 months). This badge levels up to bronze, silver and gold with each additional 12 levels thereafter (level 12 = basic, 24 = bronze, 36 = silver and 48 = gold).<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Is the badge granted automatically?</i><br>No. Please send an email with a link to your Steam profile and the level reached to the following email address: translationserver@valvesoftware.com<br><br>
							<li><i>Q: Is there any other gain by submitting translations to enter the Core Team of my language? </i><br>While one can get a Translator's Badge by reaching level 12, STS is otherwise entirely voluntary and your main gain should be delivering the best translation you and your team can offer for users of your language.<br><br>
						</ul>
