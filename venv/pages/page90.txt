<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">IceFrog</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p><b>IceFrog</b> (born 1983&#32;or&#32;1984)<sup id="cite_ref-dotablog2009_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dotablog2009-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">pseudonymous</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game_designer" class="mw-redirect" title="Video game designer">video game designer</a> known for being the lead designer of <i><a href="/wiki/Defense_of_the_Ancients" title="Defense of the Ancients">Defense of the Ancients</a></i> (DotA), a custom <i><a href="/wiki/Warcraft_III:_Reign_of_Chaos" title="Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos">Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Mod_(video_gaming)" title="Mod (video gaming)">mod</a>, as well as for the stand-alone sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Dota_2" title="Dota 2">Dota 2</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His involvement with <i>Defense of the Ancients</i> originally began in 2005, when he inherited the reins of the dominant variant entitled <i>DotA Allstars</i> from "Neichus", who himself inherited it from Steve "Guinsoo" Feak.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Identity">Identity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IceFrog&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Identity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>IceFrog is noted for his continued anonymity, having never publicly disclosed his identity.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In February 2009, IceFrog revealed on his blog that he was 25 years old at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-dotablog2009_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dotablog2009-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, an anonymous person who claimed to be a Valve employee wrote a blog post titled "The Truth About IceFrog", in which he claims that IceFrog had previously worked secretly on <i><a href="/wiki/Heroes_of_Newerth" title="Heroes of Newerth">Heroes of Newerth</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/S2_Games" title="S2 Games">S2 Games</a> before joining Valve, as well as stating his identity as Abdul Ismail.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> An April 2017 court document regarding the ownership of the <i>Dota</i> intellectual property reaffirmed Ismail as the identity of IceFrog, as well as confirming his pre-Valve contributions in <i>Heroes of Newerth</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
