<p><b>Video game design</b> is the process of designing the content and rules of a <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Game_development#Pre-production" class="mw-redirect" title="Game development">pre-production stage</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and designing the gameplay, environment, storyline, and characters in the <a href="/wiki/Video_game_development#Production" title="Video game development">production stage</a>. The designer of a game is very much like the director of a film; the designer is the visionary of the game and controls the artistic and technical elements of the game in fulfillment of their vision.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Video game design requires artistic and technical competence as well as writing skills.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As the industry has aged and embraced alternative production methodologies such as agile, the role of a principal game designer has begun to separate - some studios emphasising the auteur model while others emphasising a more team oriented model. Within the <a href="/wiki/Video_game_industry" title="Video game industry">video game industry</a>, video game design is usually just referred to as "<a href="/wiki/Game_design" title="Game design">game design</a>", which is a more general term elsewhere.
</p><p>Video game programmers have also sometimes comprised the entire design team. This is the case of such noted designers as <a href="/wiki/Sid_Meier" title="Sid Meier">Sid Meier</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Romero" title="John Romero">John Romero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Sawyer" title="Chris Sawyer">Chris Sawyer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Will_Wright_(game_designer)" title="Will Wright (game designer)">Will Wright</a>. A notable exception to this policy was <a href="/wiki/Coleco" title="Coleco">Coleco</a>, which from its very start separated the function of design and programming.
</p><p>As games became more complex and <a href="/wiki/Home_computer" title="Home computer">computers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Video_game_console" title="Video game console">consoles</a> became more powerful, the job of the game designer became separate from the lead programmer. Soon game complexity demanded team members focused on game design. Many early veterans chose the game design path eschewing programming and delegating those tasks to others.
</p><p>With very complex games, such as <a href="/wiki/MMORPG" class="mw-redirect" title="MMORPG">MMORPGs</a>, or a big budget action or sports title, designers may number in the dozens. In these cases, there are generally one or two principal designers and many junior designers who specify subsets or subsystems of the game. In larger companies like <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Arts" title="Electronic Arts">Electronic Arts</a>, each aspect of the game (control, level design) may have a separate producer, lead designer and several general designers. They may also come up with a plot for the game.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Game_design" title="Game design">Game design</a></div>
<p>Video game design starts with an idea,<sup id="cite_ref-Bates3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bates3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> often a modification on an existing concept.<sup id="cite_ref-Bates3_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bates3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The game idea may fall within one or several <a href="/wiki/Game_genre" class="mw-redirect" title="Game genre">genres</a>. Designers often experiment with mixing genres.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The game designer usually produces an initial game proposal document containing the concept, gameplay, feature list, setting and story, target audience, requirements and schedule, staff and budget estimates.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many decisions are made during the course of a game's development about the game's design; it is the responsibility of the designer to decide which elements will be implemented, based on, for example, consistency with the game's vision, budget or hardware limitations.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Design changes may have a significant positive or negative impact on required resources.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The designer may use <a href="/wiki/Scripting_language" title="Scripting language">scripting languages</a> to implement and preview design ideas without necessarily modifying the game's <a href="/wiki/Codebase" title="Codebase">codebase</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A game designer often plays video games and demos to follow the game market development.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is common for the game designer's name to misleadingly be given an undue amount of association to the game, neglecting the rest of the development team.<sup id="cite_ref-Bates_2004,_p._162_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bates_2004,_p._162-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Funding game publishers must be taken into account, who may have specific expectations from a game<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> as most video games are <i>market-driven</i> — developed to sell for profit.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> However, if financial issues do not influence designer's decisions, the game becomes <i>design-</i> or <i>designer-driven</i>; few games are designed this way because of lack of funding.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Alternatively, a game may be <i>technology-driven</i>, such as <a href="/wiki/Quake_(video_game)" title="Quake (video game)">Quake</a> (1996),<sup id="cite_ref-AdRol51_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AdRol51-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> to show off a particular hardware achievement or to market the <a href="/wiki/Game_engine" title="Game engine">game engine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AdRol51_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AdRol51-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Finally, a game may be <i>art-driven</i>, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Myst" title="Myst">Myst</a></i> (1993),<sup id="cite_ref-AdRol52_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AdRol52-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> mainly to show off impressive visuals designed by artists.<sup id="cite_ref-AdRol52_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AdRol52-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <i>Rules of Play</i> (2004), Katie Salen and Eric Zimmermann write:
</p>
<table class="cquote pullquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tbody><tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: &#39;Times New Roman&#39;, Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“
</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">A game designer is a particular kind of designer, much like a graphic designer, industrial designer, or architect.  A game designer is not necessarily a programmer, visual designer, or project manager, although sometimes he or she can also play these roles in the creation of a game.  A game designer might work alone or as part of a larger team. A game designer might create card games, social games, video games, or any other kind of game. The focus of a game designer is designing game play, conceiving and designing rules and structures that result in an experience for players.
<p>Thus game design, as a discipline, requires a focus on games in and of themselves. Rather than placing games in the service of another field such as sociology, literary criticism, or computer science, our aim is to study games within their own disciplinary space. Because game design is an emerging discipline, we often borrow from other areas of knowledge — from mathematics and cognitive science; from semiotics and cultural studies. We may not borrow in the most orthodox manner, but we do so in the service of helping to establish a field of game design proper.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_designer">Game designer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Game designer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A game designer is a person who designs <a href="/wiki/Gameplay" title="Gameplay">gameplay</a>, conceiving and designing the rules and structure of a <a href="/wiki/Game" title="Game">game</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Many designers start their career in testing departments, other roles in game development or in classroom conditions,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> where mistakes by others can be seen first-hand.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Lead designer</b> coordinates the work of other designers and is the main visionary of the game.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bethke40_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bethke40-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Lead designer ensures team communication, makes large design decisions, and presents design outside of the team.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Often the lead designer is technically and artistically astute.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Keeping well-presented documentation also falls within the lead designer responsibilities.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Lead designer may be the founder of a game development company or a promoted employee.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Game_mechanics" title="Game mechanics">Game mechanics</a> designer</b> or <b>systems designer</b> designs and <a href="/wiki/Game_balance" title="Game balance">balances</a> the game's rules.<sup id="cite_ref-Bethke40_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bethke40-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Level_designer" class="mw-redirect" title="Level designer">Level designer</a></b> or <b>environment designer</b> is a position becoming prominent in the recent years.<sup id="cite_ref-Bates_2004,_p._162_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bates_2004,_p._162-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Level designer is the person responsible for creating game environment, <a href="/wiki/Level_(video_gaming)" title="Level (video gaming)">levels</a>, and missions.<sup id="cite_ref-MoNov76_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MoNov76-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ShahArt1_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ShahArt1-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Oxland296297_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oxland296297-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Compensation">Compensation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Compensation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, a game designer with more than six years of experience earned an average of <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>65,000 (GBP <span style="white-space: nowrap">GB£44,761.22</span>), $54,000 (GBP <b>£</b>37,186.24) with three to six years of experience and $44,000 (GBP  <b>£</b>30,299.90) with less than 3 years of experience. Lead designers earned $75,000 (GBP <b>£</b>51,647.56)  with three to six years of experience and $95,000(GBP <b>£</b>65,420.24) with more than six years of experience.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, a game designer with less than 3 years of experience earned, on average, $55,000 (GBP <b>£</b>37,874.88). A game designer with more than 6 years of experience made, on average, $105,000 (GBP <b>£</b>72,306.58). The average salary of these designers varies depending on their region.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> As of 2015 the salary of experienced workers has shifted to approximately $87,000 USD (GBP <b>£</b>59,911.17) <sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Disciplines">Disciplines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Disciplines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_design">World design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: World design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>World design is the creation of a backstory, setting, and theme for the game; often done by a lead designer. <sup id="cite_ref-BrSch5_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BrSch5-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> World design can also be the creation of a universe or a map, as well as topics or areas that are likely to be pursued by the player. It is a map referenced for creation of everything as it shows where it is and allows for the most logistical design in any given game.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="System_design">System design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: System design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>System design is the creation of game rules and underlying mathematical patterns.<sup id="cite_ref-BrSch5_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BrSch5-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Content_design">Content design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Content design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Content design is the creation of characters, items, puzzles, and missions.<sup id="cite_ref-BrSch5_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BrSch5-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A secondary definition of Content design is the creation of any aspect of the game that is not required for the game to function properly and meet the <a href="/wiki/Minimum_viable_product" title="Minimum viable product">minimum viable product</a> standard. In essence, content is the complexity added to a minimum viable product to increase its value. An example of this is the item list from Final Fantasy. None of the items are necessary for the game to function, but they add value and complexity to the game as a whole.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_writing">Game writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Game writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Video_game_writing" title="Video game writing">Video game writing</a></div>
<p>Game writing involves writing dialogue, text, and story.<sup id="cite_ref-BrSch5_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BrSch5-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Writing in games also includes the elements in which the literature is presented. Voice acting, text, and music are all elements of game writing.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Level_design">Level design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Level design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Level_design" title="Level design">Level design</a></div>
<p>Level design is the construction of world levels and its features.<sup id="cite_ref-MoNov76_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MoNov76-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ShahArt1_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ShahArt1-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Oxland296297_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oxland296297-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BrSch5_40-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BrSch5-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Level design makes use of many different fields to create a game world. Lighting, space, framing, color and contrast are used to draw a player's attention. A designer can then use these elements to guide or direct the player in a specific direction through the game world, or mislead them
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="User_interface_design">User interface design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: User interface design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>User interface (UI) design deals with the construction the user interactions and feedback interface, like <a href="/wiki/Menu_(computing)" title="Menu (computing)">menus</a> or <a href="/wiki/HUD_(video_gaming)" class="mw-redirect" title="HUD (video gaming)">heads-up displays</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BrSch5_40-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BrSch5-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The user interface also incorporates game mechanics design. Deciding how much information to give the player and in what way allows the designer to inform the player about the world, or perhaps leave them uninformed. Another aspect to consider is the method of input a game will use and deciding to what degree a player can interact with a game with these inputs. These choices have a profound effect on the mood of the game, as it directly affects the player in both noticeable and subtle ways.
</p><p>User interface design in video games has unique goals. A conscious decision has to be made regarding the amount of information to relay to the player. However, the UI in games do not have to be absolutely streamlined. Players expect challenges and are willing to accept them as long as the experience is sufficiently rewarding. By the same token, navigating or interaction with a game's UI can be satisfying without the need to be effortless.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Audio_design">Audio design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Audio design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Audio design involves the process of creating or incorporating all of the sounds that are in the game, like <a href="/wiki/Sound_effects" class="mw-redirect" title="Sound effects">sound effects</a> or <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting" title="Voice acting">voice acting</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_feel">Game feel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Game feel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The disciplines listed above all combine to form the discipline of <a href="/wiki/Game_feel" title="Game feel">game feel</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_elements">Game elements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Game elements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Narrative">Narrative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Narrative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Narrative_designer" title="Narrative designer">Narrative designer</a></div>
<p>Numerous games have narrative elements which give a context to an event in a game, making the activity of playing it less abstract and enhance its entertainment value, although narrative elements are not always clearly present or present at all. The original version of <i><a href="/wiki/Tetris" title="Tetris">Tetris</a></i> is an example of a game apparently without narrative. Some<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (March 2010)">who?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <a href="/wiki/Narratology" title="Narratology">narratologists</a> claim that all games have a narrative element. Some go further and claim that games are essentially a form of narrative. Narrative in practice can be the starting point for the development of a game, or can be added to a design that started as a set of game mechanics.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Gameplay" title="Gameplay">Gameplay</a></div>
<p>Gameplay is the interactive aspects of video game design. Gameplay involves player interaction with the game, usually for the purpose of entertainment, education or training.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_process">Design process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Video_game_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Design process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The design process varies from designer to designer and companies have different formal procedures and philosophies.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The typical "textbook" approach is to start with a concept or a previously completed game and from there create a <a href="/wiki/Game_design_document" title="Game design document">game design document</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> This document is intended to map out the complete game design and acts as a central resource for the development team. This document should ideally be updated as the game evolves throughout the production process.
</p><p>Designers are frequently expected to adapt to multiple roles of widely varying nature: For example, concept prototyping can be assisted with the use of pre-existing engines and tools like <a href="/wiki/GameMaker_Studio" title="GameMaker Studio">GameMaker Studio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unity_(game_engine)" title="Unity (game engine)">Unity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Godot_(game_engine)" title="Godot (game engine)">Godot</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Construct_(game_engine)" title="Construct (game engine)">Construct</a>. Level designs might be done first on paper and again for the game engine using a 3D modelling tool. Scripting languages are used for many elements—AI, cutscenes, GUI, environmental processes, and many other behaviours and effects—that designers would want to tune without a programmer's assistance. Setting, story and character concepts require a research and writing process. Designers may oversee focus testing, write up art and audio asset lists, and write game documentation. In addition to the skillset, designers are ideally clear communicators with attention to detail and ability to delegate responsibilities appropriately.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Design approval<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="first mention (March 2010)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> in the commercial setting is a continuous process from the earliest stages until the game ships.
</p><p>When a new project is being discussed (either internally, or as a result of dialogue with potential publishers), the designer may be asked to write a sell-sheet of short concepts, followed by a one or two-page pitch of specific features, audience, platform, and other details. Designers will first meet with leads in other departments to establish agreement on the feasibility of the game given the available time, scope, and budget. If the pitch is approved, early milestones focus on the creation of a fleshed-out design document. Some developers advocate a prototyping phase before the design document is written to experiment with new ideas before they become part of the design.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (March 2010)">original research?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>As production progresses, designers are asked to make frequent decisions about elements missing from the design. The consequences of these decisions are hard to predict and often can only be determined after creating the full implementation. These are referred to as the <i>unknowns</i> of the design, and the faster they are uncovered, the less risk the team faces later in the production process. Outside factors such as budget cuts or changes in milestone expectations also result in cuts to the design, and while overly large cuts can take the heart out of a project, cuts can also result in a streamlined design with only the essential features, polished well.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (March 2010)">original research?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Towards the end of production, designers take the brunt of responsibility for ensuring that the gameplay remains at a uniform standard throughout the game, even in very long games. This task is made more difficult under "crunch" conditions, as the entire team may begin to lose sight of the core gameplay once pressured to hit a date for a finished and bug-free game.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (March 2010)">original research?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
